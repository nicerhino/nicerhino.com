Development

Your problem

How can we take on new work when our devs are so busy keeping the lights on?

As your business grows, your technical team can find themselves operating beyond their capacity. Growing customer demand expands your list of defect reports, and taking on more staff increases the need for automation. All that extra load on your systems causes outages. And all this pressure means you're too busy to train new staff.

Our solution

Nice Rhino can help you alleviate immediate capacity and knowledge constraints in software development, DevOps and automation, and cloud infrastructure. We want to partner with you to develop solutions transparently and collaboratively, avoiding the need for ongoing professional services.

Your problem

I have trouble ascertaining what our tech team does week to week.

As technical teams grow, their ad hoc organisational style becomes more difficult to manage. A growing number of staff and customers are relying on them to deliver projects and fix bugs, but that only exacerbates the issue.

Our solution

Nice Rhino can help you leverage industry standard frameworks, like Agile, to better coordinate your technical resources. We can help you build teams that self-organise around a problem, take ownership of solutions and empower team members to communicate more effectively with stakeholders.

Your problem

In small businesses, the tech lead is the gatekeeper for all technical delivery. However as tech leads become CTOs, they can become overwhelmed as they try to balance business demands with technical goals and budgetary constraints. Getting the attention of IT can often become a popularity contest.

Enablement

Your problem

Consultancies just throw things over the fence, and we're left to do all the hard work. Why talk to you?

Yep, we agree. Having a consultancy deliver a solution behind closed doors, then toss it back and leave, is a time honoured consulting strategy. It's low risk, high reward work, and is also a great way to force customers onto the never-ending treadmill of professional services.

Our solution

Our approach to consulting is centred around what we call enablement, which in other words is “teaching a person to fish”. We actively avoid establishing long term relationships of dependence. Instead we seek to empower your existing teams to carry on the work we started, so you can become self-reliant.
